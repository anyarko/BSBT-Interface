Sex Trafficking Prevalence Project: Privacy Notice and Informed Consent


Who are we?

We are a group of researchers at the University of Nottingham studying sex trafficking in England and around the world. For this study, we are seeking to understand the prevalence of sex trafficking of men and boys in different areas of England. Currently there is no reliable baseline data about the prevalence of sex trafficking of men and boys in England. Consequently, it is difficult to judge whether government policies and local support services are effectively utilising resources to target men and boys in the most ‘at risk’ areas. Given the known risk to women and girls, men and boy survivors are often overlooked. Therefore, this study aims to rank regions in England by rate of sex trafficking of men and boys.

It is important to note that the research team acknowledge the difference between those who identify as sex workers and those who identify as having been sex trafficked. This research project aims to understand the prevalence of sex trafficking of men and boys, and not the prevalence of sex work by men and boys.

Why are you being asked to take part in this research?

We want to record your views about different regions of England. You are being asked because you live and/or work or study in England. You may also be an expert in this area, but we are also interested in your opinions even if you don’t work in this area. Our research method relies on the “wisdom of the crowd”, so we want to hear from everyone.

What will happen to the data you give

We are not asking for any personal information from you that could be used to identify you. Your answers will be anonymous. We will process the data you do give us about your perception of the prevalence and risk of sex trafficking in different regions in England in line with the University’s data-handling policies. We will keep it for as long as we are working on this topic. We will publish the dataset in an open-access repository so other researchers can use it too in future.

By clicking “I consent” you are agreeing to this use of the data you give us.

Many thanks for taking the time to complete our survey on this important topic. Your answers will help us understand the prevalence of sex trafficking of men and boys in different areas of England, and hopefully build towards effective means of ending it by 2030 in line with the Sustainable Development Goals.
